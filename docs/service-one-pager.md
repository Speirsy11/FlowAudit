# SMB AI Workflow Audit — Local Service One-Pager

## Positioning

A fixed-scope, safety-first workflow audit for small local businesses that want practical AI/automation ideas without handing over sensitive systems or betting the business on a chatbot.

This is currently a **fictional/ethical validation package**, not an active public offer. It uses fictional sample data and redacted-style examples only until Charlie explicitly approves real outreach, client conversations, payment setup, or delivery work.

## Who it is for

Best fit:

- Owner-operated local service businesses with repetitive admin work.
- Teams handling bookings, quotes, follow-ups, invoices, FAQs, or internal checklists.
- Businesses curious about AI but worried about privacy, accuracy, or staff disruption.

Poor fit:

- Regulated workflows needing specialist legal/compliance advice.
- Requests to access inboxes, CRMs, accounting tools, customer records, payroll, medical, or legal data during discovery.
- Owners wanting fully autonomous customer messaging or guaranteed cost savings.

## Promise

In one lightweight audit, the business receives:

1. A simple map of 5–8 repetitive workflows.
2. A scored shortlist of automation candidates.
3. The top 3 low-risk opportunities.
4. One recommended draft-only pilot that keeps a human in the loop.
5. Risk notes, rollback steps, non-goals, and approval gates.

The audit **does not guarantee ROI**, compliance, accuracy, revenue growth, or successful implementation. It is a decision-support deliverable for safer experimentation.

## Delivery shape

- **Intake:** 30–45 minute discovery conversation using redacted examples only.
- **Review:** Manual workflow mapping with no direct system access by default.
- **Report:** Short written audit report using the included template.
- **Pilot recommendation:** One reversible, human-reviewed draft-only pilot.
- **Follow-up:** Optional review call to decide whether any next step is worth approving.

Systems accessed: none by default. If this ever moves beyond fictional validation, any real system review requires explicit written permission, minimum necessary access, and a privacy-safe plan first.

## Example first pilots

- Draft-only quote follow-up email template generated from a manually supplied, redacted job summary.
- Internal checklist for booking handovers between staff.
- FAQ answer draft library for common customer questions, reviewed by a human before use.
- Weekly admin summary assembled from manually provided non-sensitive notes.

No customer messages should be sent automatically.

## Safety boundaries

- Do not collect passwords, API keys, bank details, customer records, payroll data, medical data, or legal case details.
- Do not scrape inboxes, drives, CRMs, accounting tools, or websites without explicit written authorization and a privacy-safe plan.
- Do not promise revenue, savings, compliance, or guaranteed accuracy.
- Keep automations reversible; include rollback or deactivation steps.
- Prefer fictional/redacted examples, manual review, and draft-only outputs.

## Validation goal

The next approved business step would be to validate whether owners understand and value this offer before building tooling. That validation can happen with a reviewed landing page, private conversations, or a small waitlist only after explicit approval. Until then, this repo remains a local-only preparation artifact.
