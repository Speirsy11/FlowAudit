# SMB AI Workflow Audit Kit — Buyer-Fit Checklist

> Local review aid only. This is not approval to contact anyone, qualify a real lead, collect business data, publish, push, sell, invoice, create a form/waitlist/payment page, inspect systems, deploy anything, or run a live audit.

Use this checklist to decide whether the fictional offer has a clear buyer profile before any approval-gated validation step.

## Good-fit signals

A future buyer may be a good fit if most of these are true:

- Owner-operated or small team with repeated admin workflows.
- Workflows are easy to describe without showing private systems or customer exports.
- The team already reviews customer-facing work manually.
- The first useful outcome could be a report, checklist, draft library, or workflow map rather than live automation.
- The business has clear examples of repeated quote replies, booking handovers, FAQs, follow-ups, intake triage, or internal checklists.
- The buyer wants practical decision support, not a vague AI transformation project.
- The buyer accepts that any pilot is draft-only, reversible, and human-reviewed.
- The buyer can say what would make a recommendation unsafe or not worth pursuing.

## Poor-fit / stop signals

Do not proceed without a rethink if the buyer or workflow requires:

- passwords, API keys, admin access, private exports, unrestricted inbox/drive/CRM/accounting access, or customer databases;
- bank, payroll, tax, legal, medical, insurance, credit, immigration, safeguarding, or other regulated/high-stakes records;
- guaranteed ROI, savings, revenue, compliance, accuracy, or turnaround claims;
- automatic customer messages, production system writes, or live integrations as the first step;
- broad AI strategy work with no specific repeated workflow;
- cold outreach at scale, scraping, ads, public claims, payment setup, or a waitlist before the package is approved;
- urgency or pressure to skip written permission, data boundaries, human review, or rollback planning.

## Buyer-readiness questions

For local review only, answer these with fictional or generalized examples:

1. What repeated workflow causes visible friction each week?
2. Can the workflow be explained without sharing private/customer data?
3. Who currently reviews the output before it reaches a customer or system?
4. What would a useful report change: decision clarity, manual process, pilot scope, or stop decision?
5. Which data must stay out of scope by default?
6. What would make the workflow unsafe to automate?
7. What is the smallest draft-only pilot that could be reviewed manually?
8. What rollback/deactivation step would stop the pilot immediately?

## Fit scoring

Score each area 0, 1, or 2.

| Area | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Repeated workflow | Vague or rare | Some repetition | Frequent, concrete workflow |
| Safe data boundary | Needs private data | Can redact with effort | Easy to use generalized/fictional examples |
| Human review | No reviewer | Informal reviewer | Clear reviewer and approval step |
| First deliverable value | Generic advice | Some clarity | Concrete report/checklist/decision support |
| Pilot safety | Needs live access | Draft-only with caveats | Draft-only, reversible, no live access |

## Interpretation

- **8-10:** strong local-fit candidate for a later approval-gated validation conversation.
- **5-7:** refine scope before any validation; tighten workflow, data boundary, or reviewer.
- **0-4:** poor fit for this offer; park unless the workflow changes.

Any stop signal overrides the score.

## Safe validation shape later

If Charlie explicitly approves a future validation conversation, keep it narrow:

- one named person/business only;
- exact wording approved in advance;
- no private files, exports, credentials, screenshots, records, or system access;
- ask only about pain, clarity, perceived usefulness, and what feels unsafe;
- stop if the conversation turns into implementation, live diagnosis, regulated advice, or data collection.
