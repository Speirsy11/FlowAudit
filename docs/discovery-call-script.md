# SMB AI Workflow Audit — Discovery Call Script

Use this script for future validation calls only after explicit approval to contact people. Do not use it for outreach, email, messages, public posts, sales calls, or customer contact without Charlie's approval.

## Purpose

Validate whether a small-business owner understands and values a safety-first AI workflow audit. The call should not collect secrets, access real systems, or diagnose regulated/compliance-sensitive work.

## Pre-call reminder

- This is discovery, not implementation.
- Plain-language rule: do not collect secrets or private customer/financial records.
- Ask for fictional, generalized, or redacted-style examples only.
- Do not request passwords, API keys, customer records, bank details, payroll data, medical data, legal case details, or exports from private systems.
- Systems accessed: none.
- No customer messages should be sent automatically.

## Opening

> Thanks for taking the time. I am testing whether a fixed-scope AI workflow audit would be useful for small businesses. I am not asking for access to any systems today. Please keep examples general or redacted, and do not share private customer or financial details.

## Qualification questions

1. What kind of business do you run, and roughly how many people handle admin or customer communication?
2. Which repetitive tasks eat the most owner/staff time each week?
3. Where do handoffs or mistakes happen most often?
4. What have you already tried: templates, spreadsheets, Zapier/n8n, ChatGPT, VA support, CRM automations, or nothing yet?
5. What would make AI/automation feel unsafe or annoying for your team?
6. Which systems are involved at a high level? For example: email, booking calendar, spreadsheet, website forms, CRM, accounting tool. Do not share access or exports.

## Workflow mapping prompts

For each candidate workflow, ask:

- Trigger: what starts the task?
- Input: what information is needed?
- Decision: what judgement is required?
- Output: what gets created or updated?
- Human review: who checks it today?
- Failure mode: what happens if it is wrong?
- Frequency: how often does it happen?
- Pain: why does it matter?

## Offer validation questions

1. If you received a short report mapping your top workflows and ranking the safest first automation opportunities, would that be useful?
2. Would a draft-only pilot recommendation feel safer than an end-to-end automation build?
3. What would you expect to see in the report before trusting it?
4. What price would feel obviously too cheap, reasonable, and too expensive for a fixed-scope audit?
5. Who else would need to approve an audit or implementation?
6. What would make you say no?

## Close

Summarize back:

- Top 2–3 workflow pains.
- Main safety concerns.
- Whether the audit report would be valuable.
- Any objections around price, trust, privacy, or implementation.

Then say:

> I will not contact your systems or customers from this call. If this becomes a real offer, the first deliverable would be a written report with risks, non-goals, human-review points, and rollback steps before any implementation.

## Red flags

Stop or redirect if the person asks for:

- Autonomous customer messaging without human review.
- Access to private inboxes, CRMs, accounting tools, customer files, or credentials during discovery.
- Work involving medical, legal, payroll, immigration, credit, insurance, tax, or other regulated decisions without specialist review.
- A guarantee of savings, revenue, compliance, or accuracy.
