## Headline

Find the safest first place to use AI in your small business.

## Subheadline

A fixed-scope workflow audit for owner-operated businesses that want practical automation ideas without handing over private systems or letting AI message customers on its own.

## Who this is for

- You lose time to repeated quote replies, booking admin, handovers, FAQs, follow-ups, or internal checklists.
- You are curious about AI but worried about privacy, weird outputs, customer trust, or staff disruption.
- You want a plain-English report before deciding whether to build anything.

## What you get

- A simple map of your repetitive workflows.
- A scored shortlist of the safest automation candidates.
- The top 3 practical opportunities.
- One recommended draft-only pilot with human review.
- Risk notes, non-goals, rollback steps, and approval gates.

## Safety-first by default

- Systems accessed: none by default.
- Discovery uses generalized, fictional, or redacted-style examples.
- No passwords, API keys, bank details, payroll records, medical/legal data, or private customer exports requested.
- No customer messages should be sent automatically.
- No promises of ROI, savings, revenue, compliance, or perfect accuracy.

## Example first pilots

- A draft-only quote follow-up that asks for missing details.
- A booking handover checklist for staff.
- A reviewed FAQ draft library for common customer questions.
- A weekly admin summary from manually supplied non-sensitive notes.

## How it works

1. Short discovery call using safe, generalized examples.
2. Manual workflow mapping and scoring.
3. Written report with recommended first pilot and risk controls.
4. Optional review call to decide whether any next step is worth approving.

## FAQ

### Will you need access to my inbox, CRM, accounting tool, or customer files?

No, not for the audit by default. The first version works from a conversation and redacted/manual examples. Any real system review would require a separate written permission and data-handling plan.

### Will AI send messages to my customers?

No. The recommended first pilots are draft-only and require human review before anything is sent.

### Is this a guarantee that I will save money?

No. The audit is a decision-support report, not a financial guarantee. It highlights likely opportunities and risks so you can choose whether to continue.

### What businesses are a poor fit?

Regulated or high-stakes workflows involving legal, medical, payroll, tax, credit, insurance, immigration, or similar decisions need specialist review and are not a fit for a lightweight first audit.

### What happens after the report?

You can stop, tighten the process manually, or separately approve a small implementation plan. Implementation is not included unless scoped and approved later.
