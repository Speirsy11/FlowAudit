# Fictional sample intake — Northstar Bike Repairs

> Fictional demo data. No real business, customer, address, phone number, account, or private system is represented.

## Business snapshot

- Business type: independent bicycle repair workshop
- Team size: owner + 2 mechanics + part-time admin
- Tools used: shared email inbox, calendar, spreadsheet job tracker, accounting app, card reader, simple website form
- Main goal: reduce admin time without losing the personal customer experience

## Workflow candidates

### 1. Quote request triage

- Trigger: customer submits website form or emails repair request
- Current steps: admin reads request, checks missing info, replies with questions, adds tentative job row to spreadsheet
- Pain: 15-25 minutes per request; repeated missing details; slow during busy periods
- Safe first automation: draft a reply asking for missing bike model, symptoms, photos, and preferred dates; human reviews before sending

### 2. Job status updates

- Trigger: mechanic marks job stage in spreadsheet
- Current steps: admin manually emails customer updates
- Pain: updates are inconsistent; customers call for status
- Safe first automation: generate draft status update from approved stage labels; human reviews before sending

### 3. Invoice reminder drafting

- Trigger: completed job unpaid after 7 days
- Current steps: owner manually checks accounting app and writes reminder
- Pain: awkward and irregular follow-up
- Safe first automation: weekly report of unpaid fictional/demo invoices with suggested reminder wording; no auto-send

## Explicit non-goals

- No live access to accounting, email, CRM, bank, or customer database in this sample.
- No automatic customer messaging.
- No pricing advice or legal/compliance advice.
- No ROI guarantee.
