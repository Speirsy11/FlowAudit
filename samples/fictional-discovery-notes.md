## Top workflow pains

1. **Quote request triage**
   - Customers often omit bike model, symptoms, photos, or preferred dates.
   - Admin spends 15–25 minutes per request asking for missing details and creating a tentative tracker row.
   - High seasonal volume causes slow replies and repeated context switching.

2. **Job status updates**
   - Mechanics update a simple job tracker, but status labels are inconsistent.
   - Admin sends manual updates when customers ask, not always proactively.
   - Risk is customer frustration, not a compliance issue.

3. **Invoice reminder drafting**
   - Owner checks overdue items manually and writes reminders.
   - Tone is sensitive because reminders can annoy loyal customers.
   - This should be deferred until privacy and accounting-access boundaries are clearer.

## Current workaround

- Shared inbox labels, calendar notes, and a spreadsheet job tracker.
- A few copied email snippets, but no consistent intake checklist.
- Owner handles payment-sensitive follow-ups manually.

## Main safety concerns

- Do not connect the shared inbox, accounting app, bank, payroll, or customer database during discovery.
- Do not invent repair diagnosis, warranty terms, prices, availability, or completion dates.
- No customer messages should be sent automatically; human review is required.
- Keep the first pilot reversible with a clear rollback/deactivation step.
